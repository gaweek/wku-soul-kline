# Design Context

## Goal

Refactor the WKU soul-kline frontend into a flagship product demo for the 2026 Soul creator camp. The screen must make the single-person `Who Know U` and two-person `Who Know Us` modes feel intentional, premium, and immediately usable.

## Primary User

Young interest-social users who are curious about how they are perceived and how to begin more natural conversations. A secondary audience is contest judges who need to understand the concept and interaction quality within one or two minutes.

## Core Jobs

- Enter structured self or pair information without feeling like a long form.
- Generate a readable 18-point social K-line.
- Understand stage-level interpretation and advice.
- Switch between personal analysis and two-person resonance without losing the mental model.

## Success Criteria

- Primary action is obvious in under two seconds.
- The chart and result cards are readable on desktop and mobile.
- No visible metaphysics-sensitive framing appears in the WKU experience.
- `Who Know Us` communicates resonance and mismatch without claiming relationship certainty.
- The UI reads as a polished product tool, not a poster, horoscope, or generic AI landing page.

## Visual Direction

Product register. Restrained light interface, cool neutral surfaces, one teal/cyan accent for selected state, chart signal, and primary actions. Use compact but comfortable product spacing, clear form labels, and consistent 8-12px radii. Avoid purple mystic tokens, cream metaphysics styling, oversized decorative hero sections, and hidden text on dark backgrounds.

## Required States

- Empty state before generation.
- Loading state while analysis is running.
- Error state for generation failures.
- Generated state for both single and match modes.
- Chart hover and keyboard focus states.
