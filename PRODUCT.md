# Product

## Register

product

## Users

WKU soul-kline is for young users exploring interest-based social connection around Soul-like social contexts. They want to understand how they come across, where they are easy to understand, where they are easy to miss, and how two people can start a conversation without pressure. Judges and reviewers also need to see a complete, credible AI product demo quickly.

## Product Purpose

The product turns self-described social samples, interests, birthday-derived zodiac labels, MBTI, SBTI, current social state, and recent social friction into an actionable social connection K-line. The single-person mode answers "Who Know U"; the two-person mode answers "Who Know Us" by showing resonance, mismatch risk, stage advice, and conversation bridges. Success means users feel curious enough to analyze themselves and invite another person, while the product avoids destiny claims, superstition, medical claims, and fixed personality labeling.

## Brand Personality

Curious, precise, social-native. The interface should feel like a refined social intelligence terminal rather than a horoscope page, a generic personality test, or a heavy enterprise dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Sensitive divination-style framing, destiny prediction, and any visual language that resembles traditional divination.
- Generic AI-purple gradient landing pages with oversized hero copy and three equal feature cards.
- Overly dark dashboards where text disappears or the chart becomes decorative instead of readable.
- Task-manager language that pressures users into "missions" or "to-dos".
- MBTI or zodiac experiences that lock users into fixed identities.

## Design Principles

1. Make the K-line the product, not a decoration. Every chart point should reveal useful social interpretation.
2. Use familiar product UI patterns so users can complete the analysis without learning a new interface.
3. Keep labels and copy grounded in social behavior, not destiny or personality determinism.
4. Prefer calm hierarchy over spectacle: the demo should feel premium because it is usable, readable, and complete.
5. Treat two-person analysis as an invitation loop, not a compatibility verdict.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. All form fields need visible labels and focus states. Hover-only chart information must also be reachable through broader pointer hit areas and keyboard focus. Motion should be short, purposeful, and safe under reduced-motion preferences.
